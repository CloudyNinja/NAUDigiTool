Using the Karnaugh Map Applet

The Karnaugh Map Applet, or "KMapplet" for short is designed to be a learning aid for students.  It tests skills in translation from truth table to Karnaugh Map, as well and minimied groupings and equations.  The KMapplet can handle both Sum-Of-Products and Product-Of-Sums groupings.

The KMapplet awards points for correct answers.  The number of points awarded is based on the difficulty of the problem.  Incorrect answers will result in a loss of points.  However, if you correct the problem you will lose less points than skipping to a new problem.

 

Layout

The KMapplet has three main parts.  The information bar at the top, the main frame in the middle, and a row of buttons along the bottom. 

The Information Bar tells you what your current task is, what the current equation of selected cells is, and how many points you have.  The current task is in bold in the middle of the bar.  The number of points is located in the upper right of the bar.

The Main Frame is where the truth table and Karnaugh Map are located.  This is where you interact with the Karnaugh Map. To the right of the Karnaugh Map frame are two radio buttons. These buttons allow you to switch between "Translating Mode" and "Grouping Mode". For problems that require the entry of a boolean equation, a text area appears under the Karnaugh Map and above the buttons. Equations must be entered in proper form.

  • Translating Mode is used for translating the truth table into the Karnaugh Map. You cannot select groupings while in Translating Mode.
  • Grouping Mode is used when you want to select groupings in the Karnaugh Map. You cannot change the values of Karnaugh Map cells when in Grouping Mode.

  • SOP Equation Form: this equation is of the form ABC+DEF.
  • POS Equation Form: this equation is of the form (A+B+C)(D+E+F). If there only a single term, A+B+C is acceptable.
  • Not or Bar Notation: is marked using the ' symbol. The ' must appear before the term that is being negated. 'BA translates to "Not B and A" or B bar and A".

The Row of Buttons allow you to do various things.

  • Help takes you to a webpage where you can find information relevant to the KMapplet.
  • View Scores shows your progress through the levels, and how many points you have.
  • New Problem allows you to get a new problem of any level at any time.  Remember that quitting a problem without correctly answering it will lower your score.
  • Clear Groupings clears all groupings on the Karnaugh maps.
  • Check Work checks the correctness of your answer.  You must have done the whole problem correctly to be counted as correct.  However, you may be given hints to help you find your mistake.

 

Basic Use

Translation: When translating the truth table to the Karnaugh Map, the Karnaugh Map will default to all zero's.  To change a value in the Karnaugh Map, click the cell to cycle through the possible values. 

Grouping: Groupings are made by selecting a group of cells on the Karnaugh Map while holding down the Ctrl key.   Groupings can be made in two ways: dragging the mouse, or clicking the cells.  You must be in Grouping Mode to selecet groups.  
To select with dragging, you must press the Ctrl key and press the mouse button in the corner of an outside cell of the group you are selecting.  Then, drag the mouse until the red rectangle is aroung the group you want to select and release the mouse and Ctrl key. 
To select by clicking, you must press the Ctrl key, and then simply click the cells you want to add to the group.  A selected cell will be shaded to show that it was added to the current grouping.   When all the cells have been clicked, release the Ctrl key to select the group.
Groups may also be selected using combinations of the above styles.  Just be sure to release the Ctrl key when the group is selected before trying to select another group.
